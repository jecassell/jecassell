Untitled (Faces), 2020, Digital Phototgraphy

Untitled (Faces)

This piece is a series of eight digital photographs that have a resolution of 128 X 112 pixels. These photos were taken with a Gameboy Camera, and printed to PNG format with the use of a wifi printer (1). Iâ€™m living again in my early childhood home, and, during the extended lockdown to reduce the spread of Covid-19, I have been revisiting a lot of old memories of the house and myself.

The aesthetic of the Gameboy Camera is really unique among digital cameras. It adds an innocent, childish, whimsical, and retro-digital filter to an image from real life. Because it is from my childhood, and it brings with it innocent associations, I have used it in a past work to play with associations by taking pictures from powerful cultural events that have happened in American history, some that I have witnessed, and some that I have read about, and taking pictures of those pictures with the Gameboy Camera (2).

This work is, in a way, a continuation of that work. I went around my yard because I am spending a lot of time at home lately, and I practiced putting myself in the mindset of myself as a child. I then used the Gameboy Camera to take pictures of the faces I saw in the junk like dashboards, overturned carts, the wheel wells of forklifts and in the profile of scrap boards. The piece was very informative for my mental wellbeing and I highly recommend not taking a closer look, and instead taking a farther away look by looking for silly faces.
